Privacy Policy of the GGPoker partner site regarding personal data processing

1. General Provisions

1.1. This Policy defines the procedure for processing personal data and measures to ensure the security of personal data to protect the rights and freedoms of individuals when processing their personal data.

1.2. This Policy is a public document reflecting the Company's views on issues related to the processing of information containing personal data of individuals.

1.3. The Policy is mandatory for all employees of the Company who have access to information containing personal data, as well as individuals working with Company-owned information under concluded agreements and contracts.

1.4. The Policy applies to all personal data of subjects processed within the Company's information system using automated means.

1.5. The Company and other parties that have access to personal data are obliged to adhere to confidentiality terms regarding such data, specifically not disclosing or disseminating personal data to third parties without the consent of the data subject, unless otherwise provided by law.

2. Principles and Conditions of Personal Data Processing

2.1. The processing of personal data in the Company is based on the following principles:

• Legality and fairness of processing;
• Limitation of personal data processing to specific, predefined, and lawful purposes;
• Prevention of processing personal data that is incompatible with the purposes of its collection;
• Prohibition of combining databases containing personal data processed for incompatible purposes;
• Processing only those personal data that are relevant to the purposes of processing;
• Compliance of the content and volume of processed personal data with the declared processing purposes;
• Prevention of processing excessive personal data in relation to the declared purposes of their processing;
• Ensuring accuracy, sufficiency, and timeliness of personal data in relation to the purposes of processing personal data;
• Data must be destroyed or anonymized upon achieving the purposes of their processing or when the necessity to achieve these purposes is lost. In cases where the Company cannot rectify personal data violations committed, destruction or anonymization should occur, unless otherwise provided by law.

2.2. The Company processes personal data only with the consent of the data subject to process their personal information.

2.3. The Company does not process special categories of personal data, including data related to racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, health status, or intimate life.

2.4. The Company may assign the processing of personal data to another party with the consent of the data subject, unless otherwise required by law, based on a contract concluded with that party. Any party processing personal data on behalf of the Company must adhere to the principles and rules of personal data processing.

3. Rights of the Data Subject

3.1. The data subject decides to provide their personal data and gives consent for its processing freely, voluntarily, and in their interest. Consent for the processing of personal data may be given by the data subject or their representative in any form that allows confirming its receipt, unless otherwise specified by law.

3.2. The data subject has the right to obtain information about the processing of their personal data, unless this right is restricted by law. The data subject may request the Company to clarify their personal data, block or destroy it if the data is incomplete, outdated, inaccurate, unlawfully obtained, or no longer necessary for the stated processing purposes. Additionally, the data subject has the right to take legal measures to protect their rights as provided by law.

3.3. The processing of personal data for the purpose of promoting goods, works, or services on the market by direct contact with potential consumers using communication means, as well as for political campaigning, is permissible only with the prior consent of the data subject. Such processing of personal data is considered to be conducted without the prior consent of the data subject if the Company fails to prove that such consent was obtained.

3.4. The company is obligated to immediately cease processing the personal data of the data subject upon their request for the purposes mentioned above.

4. Ensuring the Security of Personal Data

4.1. The objectives of ensuring information security are to minimize damage from information security threats and enhance the business reputation and corporate culture of the Company.

4.2. Information is a critical asset of the Company, and protecting it is the responsibility of every employee granted access to it.

4.3. Information security involves maintaining the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of personal data.

4.4. The security of personal data processed by the Company is ensured through the implementation of legal, organizational, technical, and software measures that are necessary and sufficient to comply with the requirements of data protection legislation.

4.5. Access to information containing personal data is granted only to individuals who require it to fulfill their job duties or contractual obligations, and only to the extent necessary.

4.6. For each information resource, a designated individual is responsible for providing access and ensuring the effective functioning of information security measures.

4.7. The company has developed and approved a set of documents on personal data protection.

4.8. The company conducts an annual analysis of the measures taken to protect personal data.

5. Final provisions

5.1. Company officials responsible for violating regulations governing the processing and protection of personal data are personally liable according to the law.
